Year 2023

Ruichu Cai (蔡瑞初)

Director of the DMIR lab (Data Mining and Information Retrieval Laboratory)

I will be forever grateful to Prof. Cai, about the moral that he wanted to teach me of how to craft my research path ahead of I left the DMIR lab, by showing a story happening on a bus: Should you fight for the right of using a seat if you find a bully occupying the empty seat? — you should.

I didn’t realize the meaning until I am preparing for graduation, and need to truly fight for my future career alone: Merely bearing the virtue of sharing seats to help others is not enough; only if we as well “occupy” the seat could we be genuinely capable of showing our kindness, and share the seat to the people who need it.

Wenxin Yang (杨文新)

Secretary of Youth League Committee of School of Computer Science, GDUT

Although changing the major amounted to pull up stakes (for preparing the qualification exams) and start over, Sec. Yang has not only helped me to better keep up with the pace, but gave me great supports on several aspects such as summer college internships and foreign institution applications.

Dongning Liu (刘冬宁)

Dean of School of Computer Science, GDUT

I owe a great debt to Prof. Liu, for willing to learn about my situations (major switch) in person, never evaluating a student’s capability only by tests attributes, and always generously sharing with me how to plan for my university whenever I visit his office and talk with him.

He told me that only God knows well about everything across a scholar’s whole lifetime, but it still deserves to try hard to plan for the academic future (though is tough to constantly think of that) — Sometimes it looks as if people have already done a lot, only might just because they have planed earlier and made their first step.
